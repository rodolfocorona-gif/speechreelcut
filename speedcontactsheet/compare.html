Speed Contact Sheet vs Photo Mechanic vs Narrative Select

Three ways to cull a big shoot on a Mac. Here is how they actually differ — and where a fast, human-driven, controller-friendly approach wins.

See Speed Contact Sheet

The short version

  • Speed Contact Sheet — fast, fully manual culling for macOS at $4.99/mo or $39/yr (free trial) — about half the price of comparable culling subscriptions. You make every call; it makes those calls land faster with single-key ratings, a game controller and a built-in focus timer. Coming soon to the Mac App Store; iPad & iPhone planned.
  • Photo Mechanic — the long-established manual culler and ingest tool for Mac and Windows. Fast and trusted, keyboard-driven.
  • Narrative Select — AI-assisted culling that analyzes focus, eyes and composition to surface information as you review. Great if you want the software to flag things for you.

Which should you pick?

Choose Speed Contact Sheet if…

You cull large shoots by hand, you want the fastest possible manual workflow, you like the idea of rating from a game controller, you live in the Apple ecosystem, and you want just the essentials at about half the price — $4.99/mo or $39/yr — without paying for AI features you will not use.

Choose Photo Mechanic if…

You need cross-platform Mac/Windows support today and rely on its mature ingest and metadata tooling built up over many years.

Choose Narrative Select if…

You want AI to pre-analyze focus and expressions and surface suggestions, and you are comfortable with a subscription.

Why "human-driven" still wins for many pros

AI culling keeps getting better, but plenty of photographers still trust their own eye — especially for the emotional, in-between moments that define wedding and event work. Speed Contact Sheet is built for them: it does not try to guess your favorite frame. It simply removes every bit of friction between seeing a photo and rating it, so a manual cull of thousands of images becomes fast and even enjoyable.

FAQ

Is Speed Contact Sheet available now?

It is coming soon to the Mac App Store (version 1.0). iPad and iPhone versions are planned. See the overview.

Does it work with Lightroom and Capture One?

Yes. Because ratings are saved as standard XMP sidecars, your selections carry into Adobe Lightroom, Capture One and Adobe Bridge.
